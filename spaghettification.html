WHY IT STRETCHES

Gravity doesn't pull evenly

Gravity weakens with distance, so the side of an object nearer a black hole is always pulled harder than the side farther away. Near a black hole, this gradient gets so steep that the near and far sides experience wildly different accelerations.

THE NOODLE LIMIT

When stretch beats structure

Once the pull across an object exceeds its structural strength, it gives way. It stretches radially and compresses from the sides. Physicists literally call this spaghettification.

MASS CHANGES THE STORY

Bigger isn't always more violent

Counterintuitively, smaller black holes spaghettify you farther away from the horizon. A supermassive black hole's gradient is so gentle at its huge event horizon that you could cross it entirely intact (before dying later).

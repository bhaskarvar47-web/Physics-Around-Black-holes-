WHY IT STRECHES

Gravity doesn't pull evenly

Gravity weakens with distance, so the side of an object nearer a blackhole always pulled harder than the side farther away. Near a blackhole, this gradient gets so steep that the nearer and far sides experience wildly different accelerations.

THE NOODLE IMIT

When stretch beats structure

Once the pull across an object exceeds its structural strength, it gives way. It stretches radially and compresses from the sides. Physics literally call this "spaghettification".

MASS CHANGES THE STORY

Bigger isn't always more violent

Counterintuitively, smaller blackholes spaghettify you farther away from the horizon. A supermassive blackhole's gradient is so gentle at its huge event horizon that you could cross it entirely intact (before dying later)

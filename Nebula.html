What are Nebulas/

A Nebula a vast interstellar cloud of dust, hydrogen, helium and other ionized gases located in interstellar space, often acting as a stellar nursery where new stars are born. nebula act as bothh the birthplace and the graveyard for stars, formed either from the remnants of dyning stars(supernovae) or regions wheree gas clumps together to form a new stars. In these regions, the formations of gas, dust, and other materials "clump" together to form denser denser regions, which attract matter and eventually become dense enough to form stars. The remaining material is then thought to form planets and other planetary system objects.

Orignally, the term "Nebula" was used to describe any diffused astronomical objects, including galaxies beyond the Milky Way. The Andromeda Galaxy, for instance, was once referred as Andromeda Nebula(and spiral galaxies in general as "spiral nebulae") before the true nature of galaxies was confirmed in the early 20th century by Vesto Slipher,Edwin Hubble, and others. Edwin Hubble discoverd that most nebulae are assocaited with stars and illuminated by starlight.He also helped categorize nebulae based on the type of light spectra they produced.

They are formeed when the thin, scattered matter of the interstellar medium is pushed together by a massive gravitational disturbance, such as the shockwave from the nearby supernova explosion and powerful stellar winds shed by a dying star. The distruption causes the gas and dust to clump together, and as these clumps grow, thier gravitational pull increases, drawing in even more surrounding material. Over millions of years, the densest regions within these clouds can become so hot and highly compressed that they ignite nuclear fusion, giving birth to new stars and illuminating the surrounding gasto create the brilliant, intricately structured cosmic clouds we observe today.

Example of Nebula

Orion Nebula


Believed to be the cosmic fire of creation by the Maya of Mesoamerica, M42 blazes brightly in the constellation Orion. This stellar nursery has been known to many diffrent cultures throughout the human history. The nebula is only 1500 light years aways having an apparent magnitude of 4.0., making it closest large star-forming region to Earth.Because of its brightness and prominent location just below Orion's Belt, M42 can be spotted with the naked eye, while offering an excellent peek at stellar birth for those with telescopes. It is best observed during january.

It is a measure of the number of possible microstates, that a system can be in, given its macroscopic properties. The second law of thermodynamics states that the total entropy of an isolated system can never decrease over time, and it is often interpreted as a measure of the irreversibility of natural processes. In simple words, the second law of thermodynamics tells us that the universe tends to move towards a state of greater disorder or randomness, and the total energy of the universe is always conserved. The concept of entropy is fundamental to our understanding of the behaviour of the universe, and it has important implications for fields such as physics, chemistry, and information theory.

  • Accretion Disc

    • A black hole accretion disc is a rotating structure of hot gas, plasma and dust spiraling toward a central black hole formed by material captured by its intense gravity.Friction and magnetic forces within the disc heat thus material to extremely high temperatures, causing it to emit intense radiation across the electromagnetic spectrum, including X-rays and gamma rays. The accretion disc is a key component of many active galactic nuclei and quasars, where it can outshine the entire host galaxy. The study of black hole accretion disc provides insights into the physics of black holes, the behaviour of matter under extreme conditions, and the processes that drive the growth and evolution of black holes in the universe.

    • Accretion discs are a ubiquitous phenomenon in astrophysics, active galactic nuclei, protoplanetary discs, and gamma ray bursts all involve accretion discs. These discs veryoften give rise to astrophysical jets coming from vicinity of central black hole. The jets are highly collimated outflows of plasma that can extend for thousands of light years and are thought to be powered by the accretion disc and the magnetic fields generated by the disc. Jets are an efficient way for the star-disk system to shed angular momentum without loosing to much mass. The jets are also thought to play a key role in the evolution og galaxies, as they can heat and expel gas from the galaxy, which can suppress star formation and regulate the growth of the galaxy.

  • Schwarzchild Metric(Static Black Hole)

    The matric describes the spacetime geometry outside a spherically symmetric, non-rotating, uncharged mass.

    \[ ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 r}\right)c^2 dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 r}\right)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 (d\theta^2 + \sin^2 \theta \, d\phi^2) \]

    here ds is spacetime interval, r is radial coordinate and t is time coordinate.

  • Kerr Metric(Rotating Black Hole)

    Most of the astrophysical blackholes rotate. The Kerr metric describes an uncharged rotating black hole using mass(M) and angular momentum(J)

    . A key parameter is a=J/Mc, the spin parameter. The Kerr Metric is given by the formula-

    \[ ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{2GMr}{\rho^2 c^2}\right)c^2 dt^2 + \frac{\rho^2}{\Delta} dr^2 + \rho^2 d\theta^2 + \left(r^2 + a^2 + \frac{2GMr a^2}{\rho^2 c^2} \sin^2 \theta\right) \sin^2 \theta \, d\phi^2 \]

    The event Horizon for a rotating black hole is located at: $$r_{+} = M + \sqrt{M^{2} - a^{2}}$$
